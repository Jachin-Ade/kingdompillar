About the Chief Executive Officer

    Dr. Adetola, Adesegun Samson is a distinguished scholar and media professional with a wealth of experience in mass communication, development communication, and agricultural extension. Born in Nigeria, Dr. Adetola has built a remarkable career, marked by numerous academic achievements, professional milestones, and contributions to the fields of communication and agriculture.

    Dr. Adetola holds a Ph.D. in Mass Communication (Development Communication) from Babcock University, as well as several other degrees and certifications in mass communication, media technology, and computer science. His academic pursuits have been complemented by various professional development programs, workshops, and training sessions, showcasing his commitment to continuous learning and growth.

    Throughout his career, Dr. Adetola has worked in various capacities, including as a lecturer, researcher, and media practitioner. He has held positions at esteemed institutions such as NAERLS/ABU, Dominion University, and The Polytechnic, Ibadan, among others. His expertise in development communication, agricultural extension, and media production has earned him recognition and accolades, including the Babcock University Research Award and the Provost's College of Postgraduate Studies Thesis Scholarship Award.

    Dr. Adetola's research interests are diverse, encompassing topics such as communication strategies, media and development, agricultural extension, and public relations. His publications in reputable journals and conference proceedings demonstrate his scholarly contributions to the field. He has also presented papers at various conferences and workshops, both locally and internationally.

    Beyond academia, Dr. Adetola has been actively involved in various professional organizations, including the Nigerian Institute of Public Relations, the Advertising Regulatory Council of Nigeria, and the Agricultural Extension Society of Nigeria. His commitment to community service and development is evident in his participation in initiatives such as the National Farmers' Helpline Campaign and the Adopted Village and School Agricultural Training and Outreach Programme.

    Dr. Adetola's hobbies include traveling, reading, teaching, acting, and researching, reflecting his passion for learning, creativity, and community engagement. As a respected scholar and media professional, Dr. Adetola continues to inspire and contribute to the fields of communication and agriculture, leaving a lasting impact on his community and beyond.
